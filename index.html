The Person Behind The Polish

I grew up on the water.
This is more than a job.

Growing up in Michigan, I was on boats constantly. Family boats, friends' boats, every summer on the water. Later I worked at Grand Bay Marine, where I did everything: cleaning, winterization, repairs, and sales. I learned what boats need, what they endure, and what it really takes to keep them looking right.

When I moved to Vero Beach, I brought that same obsession with me. Every boat I work on gets the same treatment I would give my own. I scrub the spots most people skip. I condition the vinyl before the sun cracks it. I notice the grime in the corners and I deal with it. When I leave, your boat doesn't just look clean. It looks right. That is the standard I hold myself to, and it is the only standard I know.

I believe a clean boat means something. It's confidence pulling into the marina. It's pride when you fire it up on a Saturday morning. It's protecting what you worked hard for. I go the extra mile on every single job because I know exactly how good your boat can look, and I won't stop until it gets there.

"Cleanliness is next to godliness. A clean boat means ease, happiness, and time well spent on the water."
Jordan, Owner · Marine Craft Cleaners
